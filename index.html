About the Framework

FLSecLAB is a research framework for studying the security, trustworthiness, and resilience of collaborative AI systems in distributed environments such as IoT networks, cyber-physical systems, edge/cloud infrastructures, and connected vehicles.

Initially inspired by research on secure federated learning in IoT networks, FLSecLAB extends beyond federated learning to support experimental scenarios involving heterogeneous data sources, malicious or unreliable sources, trustworthiness scoring, attacks, defenses, robust fusion, and decision-making under uncertainty.

Key Features

  • Simulation of distributed collaborative AI and learning environments
  • AI-based source trustworthiness assessment
  • Trust scoring for data sources, clients, models, and decisions
  • Detection of malicious, unreliable, or low-confidence sources
  • Attack and defense modules
  • Control and reproducibility of experimental scenarios
  • Evaluation of robustness, resilience, confidence, and trustworthiness
  • Confidence-aware fusion and robust decision-making
  • Modular architecture extensible beyond federated learning

Research Context

FLSecLAB builds on the PhD work of Dr. Okba Ben Atia:

Secure Collaborative Management Platform Applied to IoT Networks

The framework extends this research direction toward secure, trustworthy, and resilient distributed AI systems, with a particular focus on source reliability, adversarial behavior, robust aggregation, explainability, and decision support in heterogeneous environments.

Applications

  • Federated learning security
  • AI-based source trustworthiness in distributed systems
  • Intrusion detection in IoT, CPS, and connected vehicles
  • Trust management and source reliability assessment
  • Trust-aware intrusion detection
  • Confidence-aware decision support
  • Attack and defense evaluation in distributed AI systems
  • Secure collaborative AI
