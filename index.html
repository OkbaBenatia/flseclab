FLSecLAB

Experimental Platform for Secure, Trustworthy, and Resilient Distributed AI

FLSecLAB is an experimental research platform for studying attacks, defense mechanisms, AI-based source trustworthiness, and resilience in distributed intelligent systems.

Created by Dr. Okba Ben Atia

📄 Paper in progress 💻 Open-source framework coming soon

About the Framework

FLSecLAB is a research framework designed to support experimentation on the security, trustworthiness, and resilience of collaborative AI systems operating in distributed and heterogeneous environments.

The platform enables the design, execution, and comparison of experimental scenarios involving heterogeneous data sources, reliable and unreliable participants, malicious behaviors, attacks, defense mechanisms, trustworthiness scoring, robust fusion, and decision-making under uncertainty.

It targets complex environments such as IoT networks, cyber-physical systems, connected vehicles, intelligent mobility infrastructures, edge/cloud systems, and distributed autonomous systems.

Key Features

  • Simulation of distributed collaborative AI environments
  • AI-based source trustworthiness assessment
  • Trust scoring for data sources, clients, models, and decisions
  • Detection of malicious, unreliable, or low-confidence sources
  • Attack and defense modules
  • Scenario-based experimentation with reproducible configurations
  • Evaluation of detection, robustness, confidence, resilience, and trustworthiness
  • Confidence-aware fusion and robust decision-making
  • Comparison of runs, baselines, attacks, and defense strategies
  • Modular architecture extensible beyond federated learning

Research Context

FLSecLAB builds on the PhD work of Dr. Okba Ben Atia:

Secure Collaborative Management Platform Applied to IoT Networks

The framework extends this research direction toward secure, trustworthy, and resilient distributed AI systems, with a particular focus on source reliability, adversarial behavior, robust aggregation, explainability, confidence-aware fusion, and decision support in heterogeneous environments.

Research objective: move from simple attack detection toward a complete experimental chain: heterogeneous sources, trustworthiness assessment, robust fusion, explainable decision-making, and resilience under degraded or adversarial conditions.

Applications

  • Secure collaborative AI
  • AI-based source trustworthiness in distributed systems
  • Intrusion detection in IoT, CPS, and connected vehicles
  • Trust management and source reliability assessment
  • Trust-aware intrusion detection

  • Attack and defense evaluation in distributed AI systems

  • Resilience evaluation for intelligent cyber-physical systems

Example Use Cases

  • Evaluate the impact of unreliable or malicious sources in a distributed AI scenario
  • Compare defense mechanisms against poisoning, evasion, or degraded behaviors
  • Integrate intrusion detection datasets for IoT or connected vehicle scenarios
  • Compute trustworthiness scores before and after attacks
  • Analyze how confidence-aware fusion improves robustness and decision quality
  • Compare multiple experimental runs using unified metrics
  • Add new datasets, attacks, defenses, models, metrics, or scenarios through a modular interface
